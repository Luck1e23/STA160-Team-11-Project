Chest X-ray Classification for Multi-Label Pathologies

We study deep learning methods to automatically detect thoracic diseases from chest X-ray images. Using the NIH ChestX-ray14 cohort with 112,120 frontal CXRs and additional CheXpert scans, we fine-tune a pre-trained X-ray pathology classifier, calibrate predictions, and analyze errors across views, devices, and demographics.

Datasets

We combine NIH ChestX-ray14 with CheXpert to stress-test distribution shift, label noise, and patient-level generalization.

112k+ NIH · 224k+ CheXpert

NIH ChestX-ray14

112,120 frontal CXRs from 30,805 unique patients with 14 pathology labels. We use official train/test split and add a clean validation set.

14 labels Patient-level split Frontal CXRs

CheXpert

224k+ chest X-rays with uncertainty-aware labels. We evaluate transfer performance and calibration under label rules different from NIH.

Uncertain labels Multi-site Device shift

Test split & cohorts

We define a held-out NIH test set with balanced disease prevalence and report stratified metrics across sex, age, and acquisition device.

Stratified AUC Bias probes Calibration
Pipeline

Our pipeline integrates preprocessing, transfer learning, loss re-weighting, and post-hoc calibration to produce clinically meaningful curves.

End-to-end training

End-to-end training pipeline

1
Preprocessing & sampling

Resize CXRs, normalize intensities, and construct patient-level splits. We oversample rare pathologies while preserving cohort-level statistics.

2
Transfer learning backbone

Initialize from a pre-trained X-ray model, freeze early blocks, and fine-tune upper layers with multi-label sigmoid outputs.

3
Loss design & regularization

Use class-balanced binary cross-entropy with focal re-weighting and label smoothing to mitigate heavy imbalance and noisy labels.

4
Calibration & evaluation

Compute AUC-ROC, AUC-PR, and Brier scores per label, followed by temperature scaling and threshold selection tuned on validation patients.

Results

Our best model reaches mean AUC-ROC of approximately 0.87 across 14 labels on the held-out NIH test cohort, with consistent performance on CheXpert.

ROC · PR · Calibration
Pathology AUC-ROC AUC-PR Prevalence Notes
Atelectasis 0.86 0.43 17% Improved with class-balanced loss and data augmentation.
Cardiomegaly 0.92 0.71 12% Consistent across NIH and CheXpert cohorts.
Effusion 0.90 0.58 13% Calibration slightly overconfident at high scores.
Infiltration 0.81 0.32 19% Most label noise; benefited from label smoothing.

Metrics reported on held-out NIH ChestX-ray14 test patients. For CheXpert, we observe similar ranking performance but slightly degraded calibration, highlighting remaining domain shift.
