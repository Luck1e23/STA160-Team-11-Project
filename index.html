Chest X-ray Classification for Multi-Label Thoracic Pathologies

This project explores deep learning methods to automatically detect thoracic diseases from chest X-ray images. Using NIH ChestX-ray14 (112k images, 14 labels) and CheXpert, we apply transfer learning with the XRV Pathology Classifier and benchmark multiple CNN architectures across preprocessing, training, calibration, and error analysis.

Datasets

NIH ChestX-ray14 and CheXpert with curated splits, preprocessing, and ethical constraints.

Data curation
Patient-wise splits

NIH ChestX-ray14

112,120 frontal CXRs annotated for 14 thoracic pathologies with long-tailed label frequencies and multi-label assignments per image.

Train/validation/test splits are patient-wise to avoid leakage, with 224×224 resolution and basic augmentations.

CheXpert

224,316 images with competition labels: No Finding, Edema, Cardiomegaly, Consolidation, Pleural Effusion. Uncertainty handled via U-ignore and U-zero policies.

Preprocessing includes CLAHE, center-crop, and consistent normalization to align with pre-trained XRV expectations.

Test Set & Ethics

Held-out test sets balanced relative to label prevalence. All data are PHI-free, used strictly for academic purposes, with checks against shortcut learning.

We monitor performance across view position, device type, and sex to surface potential biases in the learned representations.

Method

From raw DICOMs/PNGs to calibrated decision scores and interpretable Grad-CAM maps.

Reproducible pipeline
Transfer learning • XRV
Preprocessing

DICOM/PNG read-in, resize to 224×224, grayscale normalization, and optional CLAHE. We create patient-wise train/validation/test splits to avoid patient leakage between sets.

Models

Transfer learning with DenseNet-121, ResNet-50, EfficientNet-B0, and the XRV Pathology Classifier. The final heads are linear layers with sigmoids for multi-label BCE training.

Training

We use BCEWithLogitsLoss with class weights for imbalance, AdamW optimizer, cosine learning rate schedules, and early stopping based on macro AUC-ROC on the validation split.

Evaluation

Per-label AUC-ROC and AUC-PR are reported alongside macro and micro aggregates. Thresholds are tuned via Youden’s J statistic, and scores are calibrated with temperature scaling.

Error Analysis & Explainability

We analyze confusions by view (AP vs. PA), device presence, and sex, and inspect Grad-CAM heatmaps to confirm that attention aligns with relevant anatomical regions, especially for long-tail classes.
